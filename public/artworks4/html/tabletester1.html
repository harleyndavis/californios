Nok Terracotta from Sujaro Gallery of African Art
The Thinker
This is one of the exceedingly rare complete pieces of ancient terracotta collected from Nigeria. This pose, with the seated male dignitary or ancestor with arms across the knees and head resting on the arms, was a favorite of Nok artists, and is often labeled “The Thinker” by contemporary scholars.

Figure of a Seated Male (complete), Nok Culture, Nigeria (tc382)

This piece was thermoluminescence tested by Alliance Science Art (Paris), and found to be authentic, having been fired (created) between 503 and 103 B.C. Its completeness and condition make it an especially important African archeological artifact.

Nok Terracotta
  The Nok terracotta image and text are from the Sujaro Gallery of African Art website and of course are copyrighted by Sujaro's owner, Andrew J. Berz. Nok terracotta is perhaps the most intriguing of west African artifacts. Thermoluminescence testing authenticates its antiquity. Nok terracotta is ancient. It predates the much sought after Benin bronzes and even the earlier very rare Igbo-Ukwu bronzes. Nok terracotta is the pièce de résistance of west African art.
