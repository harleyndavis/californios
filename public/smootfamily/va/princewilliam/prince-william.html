Princ William County Virginia, Smoot Family Association






A Compilation of Smoot Records
Compiled by  Fred Smoot and  Carole Hammett


Prince William County was established in 1730/31 from Stafford and King George Counties. Descendant Counties include Fairfax (Loudoun) in 1742; and Fauquier in 1759. The boundaries of the newly-created Prince William County were described in a 1730 Act of the General Assembly as “...above Chopawansick Creek, on Potomack river, and Deep run on Rappahannock river and a south west line to be made, from the head of the north branch of the said creek, to the head of the said Deep run... and known by the name of Prince William County.” (9 Hening 244).

25 May 1764 - 1790. Prince William County, Virginia. Edward SMOOT vs. John HALLEY, suit of ejectment filed 1769 ref land purchased by SMOOT of MITCHELL on 25 May 1764. To Dumnfries District Court in 1772, thence to Richmond General Court in 1790. Decision in favor of defendant. Land described as 300 acres originally in Stafford County, but later in Prince William, on Yocsoecaniaco Creek. (“Copies of Extant Wills From Counties Whose Records Have Been Destroyed,” by George Harrison Sanford King, Tyler's Quarterly, XXI, 1939, pp. 57-61)
